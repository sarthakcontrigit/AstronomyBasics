What is Astronomy?

  • Astronomy is the scientific study of celestial objects like stars, planets, moons and galaxies.
  • It studies the origin, evolution and structure of the universe.
  • It helps us understand our place in the cosmos.

Planets of Our Solar System

Mercury

  • Closest planet to the Sun.
  • Formed from leftover rocky material.
  • Has extreme temperatures.

Venus

  • Second planet from the Sun.
  • Similar size to Earth.
  • Formed from dense gases and rock.

Earth

  • Only known planet with life.
  • Formed about 4.5 billion years ago.
  • Has water, atmosphere and magnetic field.

Mars

  • Known as the Red Planet.
  • Once had water on its surface.
  • Formed from rocky dust and iron.

Jupiter

  • Largest planet in the solar system.
  • Made mostly of gas.
  • Formed early from hydrogen and helium.

Saturn

  • Famous for its rings.
  • Gas giant planet.
  • Formed from icy gases.

Uranus

  • Ice giant planet.
  • Rotates on its side.
  • Formed from ice, gas and rock.

Neptune

  • Farthest planet from the Sun.
  • Strong winds and storms.
  • Formed from icy materials.

Groundbreaking Discoveries

  • Discovery of exoplanets outside our solar system.
  • Evidence of black holes.
  • Big Bang theory explaining universe origin.
  • Detection of gravitational waves.

Tribute to Great Astronomers & Physicists

  • Galileo Galilei: Improved telescopes and supported heliocentric model.
  • Isaac Newton: Explained gravity and planetary motion.
  • Albert Einstein: Changed understanding of space and time.
  • Edwin Hubble: Proved galaxies exist beyond Milky Way.
  • Carl Sagan: Popularized astronomy and science education.
